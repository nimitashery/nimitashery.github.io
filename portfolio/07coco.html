    About: This tileset is inspired by the movie 'COCO' (Pixar movie). The multiscale maptiles follow the vibrant color palette of the movie with lots of purples, yellows and reds, the colors of the “Day of the Dead” celebrations and to also give-off a fun, happy aesthetic.
    As the land area follows a very vibrant color scheme, the water is kept a dark blue color throughout the different zoom levels to draw attention to the land and its various details.
    The USA cemetery point data has been used to locate all the cemetery locations in the US, represented by a red decorated altar icon.
    Marigolds are widely used to celebrate the "day of the dead", which come in shades of orange and yellow. Marigold petals are strewn on the pathway to the altar symbolizing light which provides a temporary entrance to the world of the living. Hence, yellow is used to visualize the Roads layer in this maptile, representing pathways from cities to cemeteries.
    Upon zooming towards cities / urban areas, you will notice the pink castle / tower icon used to mark city centers and on further zooming into the urban areas, the pink building footprints are revealed. A freely available font called Kamron-Bold is used for country and state labels. For other major place, road and cemetery-name labels, the Montserrat font is used.
    (Note: Due to the heavy point-data file showing US cemeteries (145,200 points), the map tends to load slowly when zooming in from a global to US-scale and may not display the US state labels accurately!)
