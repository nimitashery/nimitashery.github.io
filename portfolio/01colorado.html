    • Tourist Attractions of Colorado

    About: This was my first map project at the University of Wisconsin-Madison as part of the GIS Fundamentals Capstone Certificate program. The assignment was to design a one-page brochure template to promote tourism across each of the 50 US states. I took this opportunity to design a map showing the major tourist attractions of Colorado and learn more about my new home in the process.

    Process: The first step was to organize our data and add it into ArcGIS Pro. Then a map template was created and symbolizations were added, maintaining a clean and subtle design. A projected coordinate system is then assigned keeping in mind the projection class, case, aspect, centering, and distortions for the identified map purpose. Since this map is meant to be printed or used as a PDF, a map layout is created to continue with the design process. A second inset map is added to this layout to show the location of the state with respect to the country. Changes are made to the scale, layout, and visual hierarchy accordingly. After the content generalization, the map linework needs further simplification according to its scale. A line and polygon simplification service called Mapshaper is used for this purpose. After importing the simplified files back to ArcGIS Pro, labels, callouts, title, north arrow, scale bar and metadata are added.

    Tools: ArcGIS Pro, Mapshaper
